Your task is to provide a systematic troubleshooting plan by working through the flowchart. Follow these guidelines:

1. Start at step_0: Begin by defining the problem clearly. Identify what error messages exist, the scope of the issue (single user, all users, specific region, etc.), and when it started.

2. Work through each step sequentially: For each step in the flowchart:
- State the step name and the hypothesis being tested
- List the specific commands or actions that should be performed
- Explain what to look for in the results
- Based on the decision logic, determine which step to go to next or what resolution to apply

3. Follow the decision tree: Each step has decision criteria that determine the next action:
- If a test fails at a step, follow the "fail" path to the indicated resolution or diagnostic area
- If a test passes, proceed to the next step
- Some steps have multiple decision outcomes (e.g., step_4 has different paths for timeout, 5xx, 403/401, and 200 responses)

4. Be specific: When recommending commands, provide the exact syntax. When describing what to check, be concrete about what constitutes a pass vs. fail condition.

- What information from the issue description is relevant
- Which step in the flowchart is the appropriate starting point (usually step_0, but if the issue description provides results from early steps, you may start further along)
- What the most likely path through the flowchart will be based on the symptoms described

6. Provide context: For each diagnostic step, briefly explain why this check matters and what it rules in or out.
